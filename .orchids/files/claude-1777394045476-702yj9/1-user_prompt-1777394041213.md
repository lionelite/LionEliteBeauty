You’re too “clinical”… but not “convincing”

Saying “clinical-grade” sounds impressive—but customers today are skeptical.

👉 Competitors that win don’t just say clinical—they prove it
Example: brands emphasize clinical testing, dermatologist backing, inclusivity, etc.

What to change:

Turn:

“clinical-grade peptide skincare”

Into:

“Clinically studied peptides shown to improve skin repair, elasticity, and visible aging”

👉 Add:

“What it does in the skin”
“What you’ll actually notice”
2. No clear transformation = low conversion

Right now your messaging is ingredient-first

Customers buy result-first

You need sections like:
“What changes you’ll see in 2–4 weeks”
“Before / After expectations”
“Who this is for”

👉 Example:

Instead of:

“GHK-Cu peptide formulation”

Say:

“Helps visibly improve skin texture, reduce fine lines, and support collagen repair”
3. You’re missing emotional connection (this matters more than you think)

Even high-end skincare brands do this:

👉 They combine:

Science (logic)
Emotion (confidence, appearance, aging fear)

Right now you’re mostly logic.

Add:
“Look younger, feel confident”
“Repair the damage you can actually see”
“Support your skin as it ages”
4. No clear product pathway (this is hurting sales)

Customers don’t want to think.

Right now it’s probably:
👉 “Here’s the product—figure it out”

You need:
“Start here” section
Simple system like:

Step 1: Cleanse
Step 2: Apply peptide serum
Step 3: Moisturize

Or:

“Beginner routine”
“Advanced routine”
5. Lack of trust signals (this is HUGE in skincare)

Especially with peptides.

Customers subconsciously ask:

“Is this legit or just Instagram marketing?”

You NEED:
Testimonials (specific results)
Photos if possible
Ingredient transparency
Clear safety language

Even a simple:

“Made in USA”
“Lab-tested”
“Third-party verified”

👉 massively boosts trust
