Layer 3: Coaching Rules

The AI should follow a fixed process before answering:

Identify what the client is asking.
Review the client’s program level.
Review relevant client information.
Search the approved Lion Elite knowledge base.
Check for medical, safety or escalation triggers.
Give a personalized coaching response.
Recommend one clear next action.
Record important information in the client timeline.
Alert an administrator when human review is required.

The AI should never:

Diagnose a medical condition
Interpret symptoms as a diagnosis
Change medication
Recommend prescription medication
Provide individualized peptide dosing
Ignore stated health limitations
Invent a benefit not included in the client’s program
Promise guaranteed results
Make a major program change without recording why

4. Questionnaire Intelligence

Questionnaires should not disappear after submission. They should generate usable insight.

The system should produce an internal summary such as:

Client insight summary

Primary objective: Fat loss and improved athletic performance
Main obstacles: Inconsistent sleep, weekend eating and work travel
Training availability: Four days weekly
Important limitations: Previous right-knee injury
Lifestyle risk factors: High stress and five hours average sleep
Family-history considerations: Type 2 diabetes and hypertension
Preferred coaching approach: Direct accountability
Coach review required: Yes
Suggested onboarding focus: Sleep, meal structure and low-impact conditioning

The AI can help organize this information, but the final program decisions should be coach-approved.

Risk flags

Use three levels:

Green

No immediate concern. Program can begin after normal review.

Yellow

Coach review required before certain activities.

Examples:

Past injury
Medication affecting exercise tolerance
Very low sleep
History of disordered eating
Significant digestive issues
Recently postpartum
Multiple chronic conditions
Red

Pause automated coaching and refer for appropriate professional evaluation.

Examples:

Chest pain
Fainting
Severe shortness of breath
Suicidal thoughts
Signs of a medical emergency
Pregnancy complications
A physician has prohibited exercise
Other urgent symptoms

The app should display emergency guidance rather than attempting to coach through red-flag symptoms.

5. Monthly Call Booking

The booking card should display:

Call entitlement: 1 monthly coaching call
Current month
Call status
Book button
Reschedule button
Cancel button
Next available dates
Days remaining to use the call
Questions to submit before the call
Booking logic

Each client receives one call credit at the beginning of each active billing month.

Example:

Credit issued: August 1
Credit expires: August 31
One completed booking uses the credit
Rescheduling does not consume another credit
Cancellation before the cutoff restores the credit
Missed calls do not automatically restore it
Credits do not roll over unless an administrator approves an exception

The system should verify the client’s entitlement before showing available times. This is more reliable than simply publishing a public scheduling link.

Calendly currently supports limits on the number of times an event type can be booked per day, week or month. However, its general event-level limit is not the same as enforcing one specific client’s monthly entitlement, so our portal should maintain the individual call-credit logic and then authorize the booking.

8. Protecting Health Information

These questionnaires will collect sensitive health information. HIPAA does not automatically apply to every wellness or coaching business; it generally applies to covered healthcare entities and certain business associates. Nevertheless, Lion Elite should design the platform to a high privacy and security standard from the beginning.

The system should include:

Explicit consent before collecting health information
A clear explanation of why each category is collected
Minimum-necessary data collection
Encryption during transmission and storage
Multi-factor authentication for administrators
Role-based permissions
Separate private coach notes
Activity and access logs
Automatic logout
Secure document storage
Data retention and deletion rules
No health information in ordinary email notifications
No sensitive information in push-notification previews
A process for clients to correct or delete information
Vendor review before sending health data to third-party AI services
Signed data-processing or business-associate agreements where legally required

Health information should never be used for advertising audiences or unrelated marketing.
